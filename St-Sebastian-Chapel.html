ST SEBASTIAN CHAPEL

Overview

The St. Sebastian Chapel in Fontainhas, Goa is a charming testament to the region's rich Christian heritage and architectural prowess. The pristine white structure is dedicated to St. Sebastian who treated the ones affected by plague and other diseases.The church houses a crucifix where one can find Christ's eyes are open for striking fear into the minds and hearts of the wrongdoers. Some key features of St Sebastian Chapel include: Historical Significance: Built in 1818, this quaint chapel is dedicated to St. Sebastian. During the years of the plague in Old Goa, people prayed to St. Sebastian for deliverance from trouble caused by pests and rodents. Exterior: The chapel's pristine white facade stands in contrast to the colorful neighborhood. A striking crucifix, originally from the Palace of Inquisition in Old Goa, adorns the church. In this crucifix, Christ’s eyes are open. Interior: Inside, the chapel boasts a small nave connected to a narrow corridor. A winding staircase within the corridor leads visitors to the choir room perched above. Annual Street Feast: The St. Sebastian Chapel dominates a small square where locals used to hold a lively annual street feast. This celebration occurs each mid-November to honor the Feast of Our Lady of Livrament.

Attractions

  • Traditional Well: Attached to the chapel is a century-old well filled with water, adding to its historical charm.
  • Colorful paintings representing scenes from the Bible adorn the ceilings and walls. Statues of monks and religious leaders grace the altars.
  • Popular devotion to St. Sebastian led to the establishment of the St. Sebastian Chapel Panaji in Fontainhas, as he was known for his miraculous interventions.

Transportation

Getting to St Sebastian Chapel:

  • By train: At a distance of 2 km from Panjim Kadamba Bus Stand, 30 km from Vasco Da Gama Railway Station and 1 km from Fontainhas, lies the St. Sebastian Chapel in Panjim City of North Goa.
  • By road: If you're in Panaji, the state capital, you can: Hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the chapel. It's approximately 2 kilometers away from the Panjim Kadamba Bus Stand. If you're coming from Vasco Da Gama, it's about 30 kilometers away by road.
  • By air: The closest airport is the Goa International Airport (Dabolim Airport), also known as Dabolim Airport. It's approximately 30 kilometers from Panaji.

Getting around St Sebastian Chapel:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Horseback: Horses can be hired for around Rs. 250 per hour or they may provide you 2-3 hours sightseeing package.
  • Rickshaw: Hand-pulled rickshaws are available to hire. Walking is quicker than being pulled along in a rickshaw.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach St Sebastian Chapel and explore the town.
