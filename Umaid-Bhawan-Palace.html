Umaid Bhawan Palace

Overview

The Umaid Bhawan Palace in Jodhpur, Rajasthan, India, is one of the largest private residences in the world with 347 rooms. It was named after Maharaja Umaid Singh, grandfather of the present owner, Gaj Singh. The palace serves as the principal residence of the former Jodhpur royal family and a part of it is managed by Taj Hotels. The construction of the palace began in 1928 and was completed in 1943. The architectural style of the palace is Indo-Saracenic, designed by Er. Mohan Lal Nepalia, Budhmal Rai, and Sir Samuel Swinton Jacob, with Henry Vaughan Lanchester as the engineer.The palace was built for a noble cause, to provide employment during the famine that struck Jodhpur in the 1920s. The building of the palace employed over 3,000 locals for about 14 years, providing them with a source of income during the difficult times. The palace is spread over 26 acres of lush grounds, including 15 acres of well-kept lush gardens. The palace has an exquisite throne chamber, an exclusive private meeting hall, a Durbar Hall to meet the public, a vaulted banquet hall, private dining halls, a ballroom, a library, an indoor swimming pool and spa, a billiards room, four tennis courts, two marble squash courts, and long passages.The interior central dome of the palace sits above the sky blue inner dome, and the inner vaulted dome rises to 103 feet in the interior part, making it a major attraction in the palace. The entry to the palace has decorations of the Coat of arms of the Rathore Royal family, leading to the lobby with polished black granite flooring, and the lounge area has pink sandstone and marble floors. The principal architecture of the palace is an amalgam of Indo-Saracenic, Classical Revival, and Western Art Deco styles, with features of Buddhist and Hindu edifices such as Angkor Wat in the preparation of the layout and design of the palace.The hotel wing of the palace is run by the Taj Group of Hotels and is called the 'Taj Umaid Bhawan Palace Jodhpur. The palace museum has exhibits of stuffed leopards, a very large symbolic flag given to Maharaja Jaswant Singh by Queen Victoria in 1877, lighthouse shapes, and classic cars of the Maharajas in the garden in front of the museum. The palace is also a famous wedding venue, hosting the wedding of Bollywood actress Priyanka Chopra and Nick Jonas in 2018

Attractions

  • Museum: The palace houses a museum showcasing the elegant Art Deco design of the interior, along with exhibits of craft weapons, stuffed leopards, a symbolic flag, and classic cars of the Maharajas displayed in the garden.
  • Throne Room: The interior of the palace boasts a stunning throne room adorned with frescoes created by J.S. Norblin, reflecting Indo-Deco design at its finest.
  • Luxury Accommodations: The palace offers 70 luxurious Art Deco-style rooms and suites, including the opulent Maharaja and Maharani Suites, providing guests with a taste of royal living.
  • Architecture: The palace's architectural marvel is a blend of Indo-Saracenic, Classical Revival, and Western Art Deco styles. The intricate design features elements inspired by Buddhist and Hindu edifices like Angkor Wat, making it a visual delight.
  • Gardens: Spread over 26 acres, the lush gardens of the palace are home to dancing peacocks and a subterranean Zodiac Pool, offering a serene retreat within the grandeur of the palace.

Transportation

Getting to Umaid Bhawan Palace:

  • By Air Jodhpur Airport is the nearest airport, located approximately 5 km away from the palace. Regular flights connect Jodhpur to major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Jaipur.
  • By Train Jodhpur Railway Station is around 4-5 km away from the palace. It is well-connected to major cities in India.
  • By Road Jodhpur is well-connected by road to major cities in Rajasthan and neighboring states. You can reach Jodhpur by state-run buses or hire a taxi/cab for a comfortable journey.

Getting Around Umaid Bhawan Palace:

  • The entry fee for the museum is Rs. 30 per person for adults and Rs. 10 for kids (5-11 years old). For foreign nationals, the museum entrance fee is Rs. 100 per person.
  • The visit timing of Umaid Bhawan Palace Museum in Jodhpur is from 10 AM to 4.30 PM, and it’s open every day, throughout the year, except for public holidays.
  • Winter is the best time to visit Umaid Bhawan Palace and Museum, as the weather in Jodhpur is the most pleasant during this time.
