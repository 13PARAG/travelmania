SPITI VALLEY

Overview

Spiti Valley, nestled in the Himalayan region, is a captivating destination with unique characteristics. A mountainous cold desert freckled with green patches over a dry weather-beaten face, fascinating valleys, windswept landscapes and quiet villages. Spiti, which loosely translates as 'the middle land'. Some key features of Spiti Valley include: Mountainous Cold Desert: Spiti resembles a cold desert, marked by rugged terrain, barren mountains, and vast expanses. Its weather-beaten face is freckled with green patches, creating a striking contrast. Cultural Influence: The valley lies at the crossroads of India and Tibet, resulting in a heavy influence of Buddhism. Stark cultural similarities permeate the region, evident in monasteries, mani stones, whitewashed chortens, and fluttering prayer flags. Climate: Spiti's high altitudes maintain sub-zero temperatures. While May-June sees temperatures around 15-20°C, deep winter (December-February) can plummet to -20°C in some places. Pack warm clothing even during favorable months. Surreal Moonscapes: The valley's surreal beauty includes moonscapes, scenic Himalayan lakes, and the harsh highlands nourished by prayers. The glacial Spiti River weaves through, sustaining life.

Attractions

  • Untamed Solitude: Travelers can explore winding roads to the highest motorable village, Komic, or narrow trails flanked by stunning landscapes. Spiti offers solitude and spiritual experiences that leave visitors soulful but never fully satisfied.
  • The most famous tourist attractions in Spiti Valley are Key Monastery, Chandratal lake, Pin Valley National Park, Suraj Tal, Tabo Monastery, Kunzum Pass, Kaza, and Kibber
  • Key attractions include the monasteries of Ki and Tabo, as well as the charming villages of Kaza and Dhankar.

Transportation

Getting to Spiti Valley:

  • By train: Joginder Nagar Railway Station which is located at around 360 km from Spiti Valley. It is the nearest railway station to Spiti Valley located at around 160 km from Manali.
  • By bus: Buses are available from both Shimla and Manali to Spiti. From Shimla: A 2-day bus journey via Kinnaur Valley. From Manali: Regular bus service with stops at Rohtang Pass and Kunzum Pass
  • By car: From Shimla: Route: Delhi - Narkanda - Rampur - Kalpa - Nako-Giu Village - Tabo Village - Dhankar - Pin Valley (Mud Village) - Kaza. Distance: Approximately 235 km from Shimla to Kaza in Spiti. From Manali: Route: Manali - Solang Valley - Rohtang Pass - Gramphu - Batal - Chandratal - Kunzum Pass - Losar - Kaza - Dhankar - Tabo. Distance: Approximately 202 km from Manali to Kaza in Spiti.
  • By air: Fly to nearby cities like Shimla, Dharamshala, or Chandigarh. From there, travel to Spiti by road via bus or private cab

Getting around Spiti Valley:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Rental Cabs: You can rent jeeps or cabs to visit the beautiful spots and attraction points of the valley.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Spiti Valley and explore the town.
