Ranthambore National Park

Overview

Ranthambore National Park, located in the Sawai Madhopur district of Rajasthan, is one of the largest and most popular national parks in North India. Established in 1955 as the Sawai Madhopur Gaming Sanctuary, it was later declared as a Project Tiger Reserve in 1973 and became a national park in 1980. The park is known for its large population of tigers and other wildlife, making it a significant attraction for wildlife tourism in Rajasthan.Spread over an area of 1334 sq km, the Ranthambore National Park is divided into 10 zones, with zones 1-5 being the best for tiger sightings. The park is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including the Royal Bengal tiger, leopard, nilgai, wild boar, sambar, hyena, sloth bear, chital, and over 270 species of birds. The park's dense vegetation provides an ideal habitat for the tigers to hunt and stealthily move around, making it a prime destination for tiger tours.The park's history is closely linked to the Ranthambore Fort, which was built in 944 AD and was the territory of the famous Ranthambore tigress, Machali. The fort is now a popular tourist attraction, and the Trinetra Ganesh Temple, located inside the fort, is the oldest Ganesh shrine in Rajasthan.The best time to visit Ranthambore is from November to March, when all the safari gates are open. The park remains closed during the monsoon season, but tourists can still visit it during this time as a few gates are partially opened. The park is easily accessible by air, rail, and road, with Jaipur being the nearest airport, Sawai Madhopur being the nearest railway station, and Ranthambore being connected by road.In recent years, the tiger population in Ranthambore has increased from 59 in 2014 to 86 in 2022, thanks to government initiatives like Project Tiger and M-STrIPES. The park is not only a significant attraction for wildlife tourism but also a crucial part of India's tiger conservation efforts

Attractions

  • Wildlife Safari: Ranthambore National Park is renowned for its wildlife safari, where visitors can spot various animals such as tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and sambar deer. The park offers both jeep and canter safaris, which are conducted in the early morning and late afternoon when the animals are most active
  • Ranthambore Fort: Located within the park, Ranthambore Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the 10th century. The fort offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and is home to several temples and palaces
  • Padam Talao Lake: This is the largest lake in Ranthambore National Park, and it attracts a wide variety of birds and animals. Visitors can take a boat ride on the lake to observe the wildlife from a different perspective
  • Bakula Region: This region is known for its dense vegetation and is a great place to spot leopards and other smaller cats. The region also has several ancient temples and structures that are worth exploring
  • Birdwatching: Ranthambore National Park is home to over 300 bird species, making it a paradise for birdwatchers. The park's diverse habitats, including wetlands, grasslands, and forests, provide a variety of birding opportunities

Transportation

Getting to Ranthambore National Park:

  • By Train: The closest railway station is Sawai Madhopur, approximately 14 km from the park. From there, you can hire a cab to reach Ranthambore within 30 minutes.
  • By Flight: The nearest airport is in Jaipur, around 185 km away. Jaipur International Airport connects to major cities in India and internationally. A cab from Jaipur Airport to Ranthambore takes about 3-4 hours.
  • By Road: Ranthambore is well-connected by highways. From Jaipur, it's about 185-190 km (3-4 hours) and from Delhi, around 400 km (8 hours).

Getting Around Ranthambore National Park:

  • Jeep Safari: A 6-seater open top Gypsy that provides a more intimate experience and easy access to the park's nooks and narrow paths. It is recommended for small groups of six people or less, particularly wildlife photographers who want a clear view of big cats and other species.
  • Canter Safari: A 20-seater open top Safari bus or truck that is a very enjoyable safari style for observing wildlife. The height of the canter provides a special wildlife experience and spectacular views of the national park.
  • Hiking: Explore the Ranthambore Fort or Ranthambore ka Kila..
