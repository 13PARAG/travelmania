AGUADA FORT

Overview

Fort Aguada is a well-preserved seventeenth-century Portuguese fort, along with a lighthouse, standing in Goa, India, on Sinquerim Beach, overlooking the Arabian Sea. It is an ASI protected Monument of National Importance in Goa.The fort was originally constructed in 1612 to guard against the Dutch. It was a reference point for the vessels coming from Europe at that time. Some key features of Aguada Fort include: Fort Aguada spans an area of approximately 12,000 square meters. Constructed in 1612, Fort Aguada was originally built to guard against the Dutch. It served as a reference point for vessels arriving from Europe. The fort has strategic location on Sinquerim Beach, overlooking the Arabian Sea, made it a crucial defense against foreign invasions. Its walls, constructed using laterite stone, are up to 5 meters thick in certain sections.

Attractions

  • Aguada Fort Lighthouse: The Aguada Fort Lighthouse, erected in 1864, is the oldest of its kind in Asia.
  • Our Lady of Good Voyage: The fort houses a small chapel dedicated to Our Lady of Good Voyage, which remains in use to this day.
  • Aguada Central Jail: Part of the fort, the Aguada Central Jail, was the largest prison in Goa until 2015. Now renovated and open to tourists, it serves as a Freedom Struggle Museum

Transportation

Getting to Aguada Fort:

  • By train: The nearest railway station is in Thivim, which is approximately 20 kilometers from Aguada Fort.
  • By bus: The fort is well-connected by road, and visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus from Panaji
  • By air: Mopa Airport to Aguada Fort: Approximately 36.7 kilometers (about 60 minutes).

Getting around Aguada Fort:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach the Fort and explore it.
