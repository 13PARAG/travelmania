Kumarakom

Overview

Adorned by the magical charm of the Vembanad Lake, Kumarakom is another beguiling backwater destination in God’s Own Country. Located within the proximity to Kottayam city, this is the place where even being lazy is also one of the best things you can do during your Kerala holidays. Adorned by the charm of the emerald Vembanad Lake, the evergreen coconut grooves, breezy rice fields, and lot more, Kumarakom is popular among all the types of visitors. However, backwater cruise and houseboat stays are the most popular thing to do in this beautiful tourist destination.

Attractions

  • Kumarakom Craft Museum :- Visit this museum if you have an inclination towards exclusive antiques. This is one of the most-visited tourist attractions,if you are looking for souvenirs that hold historical as well as cultural significance.
  • Kumarakom Beach :- Kumarakom is the heart of backwaters. Here one can see everything for which Kerala is known-backwater, museums, scenery and of course beaches. After a day-long historical tour, a visit to the beach can be a rejuvenating experience.
  • Aruvikkuzhi Waterfalls :- A stone throw away from the Kumarakom, rests the ferocious beauty, Aruvikkuzhi Waterfalls. The cascading beauty is around 100 feet in length and serves as a fanatic spot for the tourists to indulge in a picnic. The waterfall is at its best when it is sunny and the chattering sound of the water when it touches craggy rocks is a treat for both soul and eyes.

Accommodation

  • Kumarakom Wood Castle Serviced Appartments :- Kumarakom Wood Castle Serviced Appartments is located in Kumarakom and features free WiFi, concierge services and express check-in and check-out. Featuring room service, this property also provides guests with a barbecue. There is free private parking and the property provides paid airport shuttle service.
  • Pearlspot Hotel :- Pearlspot Hotel features accommodation with a garden, free private parking and a restaurant. The property is set 500 metres from Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary, 16 km from Vaikom Mahadeva Temple and 17 km from Kottayam Railway Station. The accommodation offers room service, a tour desk and luggage storage for guests.
  • Rhythm Kumarakom :- Offering a 150-metre-long outdoor pool and an Ayurvedic Spa and Wellness Centre, Rhythm Kumarakom is located on the banks of Lake Vembanad at Kumarakom. Free WiFi access is available in this resort.



Transportation

Getting to Kumarakom:

  • By train: The Kottayam railway station is the nearest railhead to Kumarakom which is located at a distance of 16 kilometers. This railway station has access to various trains coming from all across the country, connecting the city to the rest of the country.
  • By bus:The most inexpensive way to travel to Kumarakom is through bus. There are many buses running from the nearby cities of Kerala. These buses are operated by the Kerala State Run Transport Corporation (KSRTC). Not only this, private air-conditioned buses also play a prominent role in connecting the city with all the major South Indian cities like Bangalore, Mangalore and Chennai.
  • By air: The Cochin International Airport is located 85 kilometers away from Kumarakom, which is the nearest one. All the major cities around the world are well connected to the city through various flights operated from this airport.

Getting around Kumarakom:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Rickshaw: Hand-pulled rickshaws are available to hire. Walking is quicker than being pulled along in a rickshaw.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Kumarakom and explore the town.
