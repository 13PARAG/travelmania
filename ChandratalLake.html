CHANDRATAL LAKE

Overview

Chandratal Lake, also known as Chandra Tal or the Lake of the Moon, is a breathtaking natural wonder nestled in the southwestern Himalayas. Chandra Taal Lake is on the Samudra Tapu plateau, which overlooks the Chandra River (a source river of the Chenab). The name of the lake originates from its crescent shape. Some key features of Chandratal Lake include: Crescent Shape: The name “Chandra Tal” originates from its distinctive crescent shape. This serene lake curves gracefully, resembling a celestial crescent in the rugged mountain landscape. Altitude and Location: Chandra Tal sits at an impressive altitude of approximately 4,300 meters (14,100 feet) in the Himalayas. On one side of the lake, mountains of scree rise dramatically, creating a stunning backdrop. Access: Chandra Tal is accessible by road from Batal and can also be reached on foot from Kunzum Pass during the late spring to early October period. The motor road extends up to a parking lot about 1 kilometer (0.62 miles) from the lake, and from there, visitors need to walk the final stretch. Flora and Fauna: Wildflower Meadows: During springtime, the banks of Chandra Tal are carpeted with hundreds of varieties of wildflowers.

Attractions

  • Degraded Grasslands: Historically, there was a plain of good grass to the north of Chandra Tal, where shepherds brought large herds for grazing. However, due to overgrazing, the grasslands have degraded.
  • Wildlife: Chandra Tal is home to species such as the Snow Leopard, Snow Cock, Chukor, Black Ring Stilt, Kestrel, Golden Eagle, Chough, Red Fox, Himalayan Ibex, and Blue Sheep. Migratory species like the Ruddy Shelduck visit during summer.
  • It overlooks the Chandra River, which is one of the source rivers of the Chenab.

Accommodation

  • Baspa Resort: Located near Chandra Taal Lake, Baspa Resort offers a comfortable stay. You can enjoy the serene surroundings and explore the beauty of the region
  • Spiti Valley Hotel: Spiti Valley Hotel provides a peaceful retreat. Amenities include free WiFi, a restaurant, and a sun terrace. It's a great choice for travelers seeking comfort and relaxation.



Transportation

Getting to Chandratal Lake:

  • By train: Joginder Nagar Railway Station: The nearest railway station to Manali. From Joginder Nagar Railway Station, you can hire a cab to reach Manali town.
  • By bus: You can utilise the buses and shared transport options running along the Manali-Kaza Road.
  • By car: Chandratal Lake is reached via a single track dirt road that diverts off from the Manali-Kaza Road near Batal.
  • By air: The nearest airport to Chandratal Lake is Bhuntar Airport, which is approximately 180 kilometers away from the lake.

Getting around Chandratal Lake:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach the Lake and explore it.
