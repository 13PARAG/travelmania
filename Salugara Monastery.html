Salugara Monastery

Overview

The renowned monastery holds a massive 110 feet tall Stupa which was constructed by Tibetan Lama- Kalu Rinpoche, even as Tibetan monks were settling down in the region during their exile. It is also known as the Tashi Homang Stupa. This Buddhist shrine houses around 5 Buddhist relics and is a place of meditation and prayer. There is a popular and ancient belief surrounding the monastery that it is guarded by the hills surrounding it. Considering its religious significance, it is a must see among the ‘Places to visit in Siliguri’. The monastery which is located 127 meters above sea level, attracts thousands of visitors each year who come to view the Stupa and the monastery. The tranquillity of the monastery which has an Indo-Tibetan style of architecture and its scenic surroundings make it an ideal spot for soul seekers and tourists.

Attractions

  • The Zang Dhok Palri Monastery:- The Zang Dhok Palri Monastery, also known as Durpin Monastery, is a revered Buddhist site situated in Kalimpong, India. Perched atop Durpin Hill, the monastery offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. The monastery is renowned for its vibrant Tibetan architecture, intricate murals, and a 108-feet tall stupa that houses sacred relics.
  • The main attraction of the monastery is the 100-foot stupa, which houses five highly revered relics of Buddhism. It is known as the Great International Tashi Gomang Stupa and is visited by tourists from around the world. The stupa is said to bring out five kinds of relics of Buddhism, making it a significant spiritual site for Buddhists and tourists alike
  • The monastery is surrounded by landscaped gardens and features colorful depictions of a meditating Lord Buddha on its perimeter walls. The interiors feature a polished wooden floor with prayer mats for devotees, creating an atmosphere of utter peace and serenity. The monastery is maintained by the Droden Kunchab Chodey Buddhist Association and is open from 5:30 am to 6:30 pm for visitors

Transportation

Getting to Salugara Monastery:

  • By train: Siliguri is well-connected by rail, with New Jalpaiguri being a major railway station in the region. From New Jalpaiguri, visitors can use local transportation services or hire taxis to reach Salugara Monastery. The monastery is located approximately 6 km from the bustling city of Siliguri, making it easily accessible by rail as well.
  • By Road:Salugara Monastery is easily accessible by road, with local transportation readily available in Siliguri. Visitors can use taxis, auto-rickshaws, or private vehicles to reach the monastery. The monastery is located near the army camp of Salugara, making it a convenient destination for travelers.
  • By air:Siliguri is well-connected by air, with the nearest airport being Bagdogra Airport. From the airport, visitors can easily hire taxis or use local transportation services to reach Salugara Monaster.y

Getting around Salugara Monastery:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Rickshaw: Hand-pulled rickshaws are available to hire. Walking is quicker than being pulled along in a rickshaw.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Salugara Monastery and explore the town.
