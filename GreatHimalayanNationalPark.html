GREAT HIMALAYAN NATIONAL PARK

Overview

This National Park in the western part of the Himalayan Mountains in the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh is characterized by high alpine peaks, alpine meadows and riverine forests.The Great Himalayan National Park (GHNP), a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a pristine natural area that spans 754 square kilometers. It encompasses an elevation range from 1,500 meters to 6,000 meters and is home to a rich biodiversity of flora and fauna. Some key features of Great Himalayan National Park include: The park was established in 1984 and is spread over an area of 1171 km2; elevations within the park range between 1500 and 6000 m. The Great Himalayan National Park is a habitat to numerous flora and more than 375 fauna species, including approximately 31 mammals, 181 birds, 3 reptiles, 9 amphibians, 11 annelids, 17 mollusks and 127 insects. Most of the Himalayan fauna has been given protection under the high priority protection category of Schedule I of the Indian Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972. The state government of Himachal Pradesh has banned hunting in the state for more than ten years. A trek of 35 to 45 km in any of the park's valleys brings one into the high elevation habitat (3,500 m and above) of animals such as blue sheep, snow leopard, Himalayan brown bear, Himalayan tahr, and musk deer.

Attractions

  • Diverse Ecosystems: GHNP boasts a variety of ecosystems, including alpine meadows, dense forests, and snow-capped peaks. These diverse habitats support a wide range of plant and animal species.
  • Endemic and Endangered Species: Within its boundaries, you'll find more than 375 species of fauna and 1,000 species of flora. Many of these are endemic to the region, making GHNP a critical conservation area.
  • Prashar Lake: One of the attractions near GHNP is Prashar Lake, located in the Mandi district of Himachal Pradesh. This serene lake sits at an altitude of 2,730 meters (8,957 feet) and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can trek to the lake and enjoy its tranquil beauty.

Transportation

Getting to Great Himalayan National Park:

  • By train: The nearest railway station to the Great Himalayan National Park is Joginder Nagar, located near Mandi. It's approximately 143 kilometers away from the park.
  • By bus: Volvo Buses: These comfortable buses connect Delhi and Manali in Himachal Pradesh. Departing from Delhi in the evening, they reach the Kullu valley (near GHNP) by morning. These semi-sleeper buses stop for dinner and breakfast along the way.
  • By car: Via Rupnagar, Bilaspur, and Mandi: This route is followed by the Volvo buses from Delhi. It takes about 10-11 hours from Delhi to reach GHNP.
  • By air: The nearest airport to the Great Himalayan National Park is Chandigarh Airport

Getting around Great Himalayan National Park:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Safari: Safaris can be booked to enjoy the sightseeing of the park.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Great Himalayan National Park and explore it.
