BASILICA OF BOM JESUS

Overview

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is one of the oldest churches in Goa and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is famous for its Baroque architecture and the tomb of St. Francis Xavier, the patron saint of Goa.Constructed between 1594 and 1605, the basilica exemplifies Renaissance Baroque and Portuguese colonial architecture.Its facade combines Ionic, Doric, Corinthian, and composite styles. Some key features of Basilica of Bom Jesus include: The Basilica of Bom Jesus is more than 408 years old and contains a modern art gallery with paintings depicting various Biblical scenes. Its interior is simple, with elaborate gilded altars, while the southern side of the transept lies the Chapel of St. Francis Xavier, where the relics of the saint are kept. The church also has paintings of scenes taken from the life of St. Francis Xavier. The basilica houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, a revered Catholic missionary.The main altar is gilded and features a statue of Ignatius of Loyola.

Attractions

  • Public Viewing of the Saints Body: The remains of St. Francis Xavier continue to attract a huge number of tourists, both Christian and non-Christian.
  • Pilgrimage Center: Bom Jesus, meaning “Good/Infant Jesus” in Portuguese, is the name used for the Ecce Homo in Lusophone countries. This Jesuit church is Indias first minor basilica and is considered one of the best examples of Portuguese colonial architecture in India.
  • Mortal Remains of St. Francis Xavier: The main attraction of the basilica is the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, a Jesuit missionary who died in 1552 while en route to China.

Transportation

Getting to Basilica of Bom Jesus:

  • By train: The nearest railway station to the Basilica of Bom Jesus is Madgaon (also known as Margao), which is approximately 35 kilometers away.
  • By bus: From Panaji, the state capital, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach the Basilica of Bom Jesus.The church is approximately 10 kilometers away from Panaji, and the journey takes about 30 minutes
  • By air: The nearest airport to Basilica of Bom Jesus is Goa.

Getting around Basilica of Bom Jesus:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Rickshaw: Hand-pulled rickshaws are available to hire. Walking is quicker than being pulled along in a rickshaw.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Basilica of Bom Jesus and explore the town.
