Sundarbans

Overview

If vistas are your thing, Bagdogra is the perfect destination for you. From offering verdant tea gardens to the majestic sights of snow-capped mountains, Bagdogra will charm and calm you at the same time. Blessed with rich flora and fauna, and the serene Teesta River, it is an ideal getaway to detox your senses. Bagdogra is also an entry point to other north-eastern states.

Attractions

  • Boat Tour of the Mangrove Forest:- Boat Tours of the mangrove forest are the only way to properly explore the depths of Sundarbans as there are no jeep safaris here. Early morning boat rides through the creeks and canals are the best time to watch the animals and birds. It is recommended that these boat tours are done in smaller groups as large groups can frighten away animals and birds.
  • Marichjhanpi:- Marichjhanpi is one of the islands within India�۪s most famous mangroves - the Sundarbans. Being the location for Amitav Ghosh�۪s novel The Hungry Tide and a Bengali movie that is due to be released in 2020, Marichjhanpi is known as an island of atrocities.
  • Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary:- Avid bird watchers can visit Sajnekhali Bird Sanctuary situated within Sundarbans National Park. Here many bird species can be observed freely within the lush greenery. Some species that can be spotted here include kingfishers, open billed storks, White Bellied Sea Eagle, Paradise Flycatcher, and the rare Asian Dowitchers.

Transportation

Getting to Sundarbans :

  • By train: The nearest major railway station is the Sealdah Railway Station in Kolkata, and the nearest local railway station is Canning. From Sealdah, visitors can take local trains to Canning and then hire a private taxi or shared public transport to reach the entry point of Sunderbans at Godkhali.
  • By Road: Visitors can either take public transport from Kolkata to Canning and further to Godkhali or hire a private taxi or cab to reach the entry point of Sunderbans. The road distance from Kolkata to Godkhali and Canning is around 120 km and 64 km, respectively.
  • By air:The nearest airport to Sundarbans National Park is the Netaji Subhash Chandra International Airport in Kolkata, which is well-connected to major cities in India. From the airport, visitors can hire a cab or a bus to reach Sundarbans, which takes around 3 hours.

Getting around Sundarbans :

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Rickshaw: Hand-pulled rickshaws are available to hire. Walking is quicker than being pulled along in a rickshaw.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Sundarbans and explore the town.
