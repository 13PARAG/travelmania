Bengal Safari Park

Overview

A Bengal Safari Park, situated within the verdant forested area of Mahananda Wildlife Sanctuary is the first animal safari park of North Bengal region. It is located on the Sevoke road at a distance of 8km from Siliguri. Bengal Safari Park was inaugurated by the CM Mamata Banerjee on 21st Jan 2016 to boost the tourism of North Bengal. Surrounded with luxuriant forest of Sal trees, Bengal Safari Park with its 700arce land gives closure toward wild side of North Bengal region. Visitors can spots wild animals like sambar deer, jungle fowl, wild boars, rhinoceros, spotted deer, and Royal Bengal of tiger during their visit of Bengal Safari Park. Presently there are two safaris conducted in Bengal Safari Park herbivore safari, and carnivore safari. With a nominal fee of 50rs (subject to change) for jungle safari and 30rs (subject to change) for entry fee, tourists can spot and observe wild animals in their natural habitat at Bengal Safari Park.

Attractions

  • Bengal Safari Park is an ideal destination for a day out with the family in the wild. The park showcases the richness and diversity of the flora and fauna of North Bengal. It also happens to be the first park in the region where animal safari expeditions were launched, which helped spur tourism in this area. Sprawled over 700 acres, the park is a part of the lush forested area of Mahananda Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • The park offers different safari rides for visitors to enjoy, such as the Tiger Safari, Leopard Safari, Mixed Herbivore Safari, and an Elephant Ride.
  • The Mixed Herbivore Safari is a 20-minute ride where visitors can see various herbivorous animals and birds

Transportation

Getting to Bengal Safari Park:

  • By train: The nearest railway station link to Bengal Safari Park is Canning, which is around 48 kilometers away. From Sealdah (Kolkata), there are frequent local trains to Canning. After reaching Canning, you can choose to travel by bus to Namkhana, Raidighi, Sonakhali, and Najat, from where motorboat services are available to reach the park
  • By Road:There is a well-laid network of roadways connecting Kolkata to Bengal Safari Park, with a distance of approximately 110 kilometers. Public transport is easily accessible, and there are additional waterway options from Sonakhali, Namkhana, Canning, Raidighi, and Najat, all at a short distance from the park
  • By air:The nearest airport to Bengal Safari Park is Netaji Subhash International Airport in Kolkata, approximately 112 kilometers away. After arriving in Kolkata, you can take a road or train route to reach the park .

Getting around Bengal Safari Park:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Rickshaw: Hand-pulled rickshaws are available to hire. Walking is quicker than being pulled along in a rickshaw.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Bengal Safari Park and explore the town.
