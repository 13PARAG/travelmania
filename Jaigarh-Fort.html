Jaigarh Fort

Overview

Jaigarh Fort, located in Jaipur, Rajasthan, is a magnificent structure built by Sawan Jai Singh II in 1726 AD on the Hill of Eagles. The fort is spread over 3 kilometres in length and a kilometre in width, making it the strongest of the three forts in Jaipur. It is known as the 'Victory Fort' because it was never conquered by enemies.The fort is made entirely of red sandstone, which is composed of dressed stone held together with lime. It houses the world's biggest cannon on wheels, known as 'Jaivana', which is 20.19 feet long and weighs 50 tonnes. The cannon has a diameter of 11 inches and is mounted on wheels with a mechanism of two back wheels mounted on roller pin bearings, allowing it to turn 360° and fire in any direction. The cannon had a range of 22 miles and used 50 kilograms (110 lb) balls.Jaigarh Fort is built with thick walls and beautiful carvings, reflecting the rich culture and heritage of the city. It houses several exquisite architectural gems such as Aram Mandir, Lalit Mandir, Laxmi Vilas and Vilas Mandir. The fort also has two ancient temples, Ram Harihar and Kal Bhairav, built in the 10th and 12th centuries, respectively.The fort's cannon foundry was one of the world's most efficient, mainly due to the abundance of iron ore mines in the vicinity. The foundry had a massive wind tunnel that sucked air from the high mountains into its furnace, creating heated air that melted the metal. The molten metal would fill a reservoir chamber and pass into a cannon mold in the casting pit. The Rajputs built a large ingenious mechanical device that had a precision gear system driven by four pairs of oxen, which was used for hollowing out the cannon barrels.The fort's armoury chamber has a wide display of shields, swords, guns, time bombs, camel-mounted guns, muskets and also a 50 kilograms (110 lb) cannonball. The fort's museum showcases a hand-drawn blueprint or image of the fort and the palace within the museum premises.Jaigarh Fort is a year-round destination, offering unique experiences every season. The best time to visit is during the cooler months of November to February, when the pleasant weather enhances the fort exploration experience. The fort is open from 09:00 hours to 18:30 hours throughout the week, but it is not advised to stay here in the evening due to the presence of wild animals in the surrounding forest.In conclusion, Jaigarh Fort is a testament to the architectural brilliance and military might of the Rajputs. Its massive cannon, Jaivana, and the ingenious mechanical device used for hollowing out cannon barrels showcase the innovation in ancient warfare. The fort's exquisite architecture, thick walls, and beautiful carvings reflect the rich culture and heritage of the city. The fort's armoury chamber and museum provide insights into the military life of Jai Singh II and the lavish lifestyle of the era. Overall, Jaigarh Fort is a must-visit destination for anyone wanting to experience the essence of Rajput's bravery and architectural brilliance.

Attractions

  • Jaivana Cannon: This is the largest cannon on wheels in the world, nearly 6 meters tall and weighing around 50 tonnes. It is a marvel of ancient warfare technology and a must-see attraction of the fort.
  • Exhibition of arms: The fort has an exhibition of 18th-century weapons and artilleries, including cannonballs, swords, shields, guns, bombs, etc. This exhibition provides a glimpse into the military history of the region.
  • Museum: The museum in the fort offers insights into the lives of past rulers and warriors, along with many photographs. It provides a historical context to the fort and its significance in the region.
  • Temples: The fort has many temples, including the centuries-old Kal Bhairav and Harihar temples. These temples are a testament to the religious heritage of the region and are worth exploring.
  • Diya Burj: This is the highest point in the fort, offering panoramic views of the surrounding area. It is a perfect spot to appreciate the fort's architecture and its strategic location.

Transportation

Getting to Jaigarh Fort:

  • Airport: The closest airport to Jaigarh Fort is Jaipur International Airport, which is about 38 kilometers away from the fort. You can take a taxi or a bus from the airport to reach the fort
  • Train Station: The closest railway station to Jaigarh Fort is Jaipur Junction Railway Station, which is about 10 kilometers away from the fort. You can take a taxi or an autorickshaw from the station to reach the fort
  • Road: Jaigarh Fort is well-connected by road to other parts of Jaipur and Rajasthan. You can take a bus or a taxi to reach the fort. The fort is located on Amer Road, which is a major road in Jaipur

Getting Around Jaigarh Fort:

  • Guided Tours: You can take a guided tour of Jaigarh Fort to learn about its history and architecture. The tours are usually conducted by local guides who are familiar with the fort's layout and can provide interesting insights
  • Walking: Jaigarh Fort is a large fort, and walking is the best way to explore it. You can walk around the fort's walls, towers, and gates to get a feel for its size and grandeur
  • Electric Carts: Some tour operators offer electric carts to take visitors around the fort. This is a convenient option if you have mobility issues or if you want to cover a lot of ground in a short amount of time
  • Cycling: You can rent a bicycle to explore the fort and its surroundings. This is a fun and eco-friendly way to get around the fort
