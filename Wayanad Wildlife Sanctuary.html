Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

Overview

Cradled in the lap of the Western Ghats, Wayanad truly deserves a top rank in the list of places to visit in Kerala. Situated at an astounding height of 700-2,100m, this stunning hill station is all about fantasy, untouched nature, unforgettable moments, and merriment! Adding more to Wayanad’s enchanting beauty, visitors can also revisit and unfold history while visiting this hilly retreat. Yes, the lush and pristine forests in Wayanad are said to be inhabited more than 3,000 years ago.

Attractions

  • WAYANAD WILDLIFE SANCTUARY:- Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary! A home to a diverse range of wildlife in Kerala, this wildlife abode takes the pride of being the second largest wildlife park in the entire of God’s Own Country. On a visit to the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, visitors can easily spot Tigers, Elephants, Panthers, Jungle Cats, Bison, Wild Dogs, Deer, and several other species of mammals. The sanctuary also has a significant population of birds, and proves to be a true treasure trove to the nature lovers and shutterbugs.
  • CHEMBRA PEAK :- The highest mountain peak in the Wayanad hill range, the Chembra Peak invites nature admirers to adore its majestic beauty along with enjoying a spectacular view of the Wayanad, Nilgiri, Malappuram, and Kozhikode districts from a height of 2100 meters above sea level. It’s a perfect weekend getaway option for locals as well as tourists for a good, half-day excursion and can also be visited by adventure enthusiasts looking to bring out the best of adventure in themselves.
  • EDAKKAL CAVES :- Edakkal Caves are considered as the most sought-after tourist destination in Wayanad that features prehistoric inscriptions and wall carvings from the Neolithic settlement. Inside the cave, you will see the human and animal figures depicted in different styles along with various objects that were used by people.

Transportation

Getting to Wayanad:

  • By train: The Wayanad nearest railway station is Kozhikode, 85.9 km away. Kozhikode is well connected by rail to major towns such as Kochi, Chennai, and Bengaluru. You can take a train to Kozhikode and then travel by road to Wayanad.
  • By bus:There is a network of good roads that lead to Wayanad from all the states. You can hire a private car and travel in comfort. Another option is to take the state transport buses that run from Bengaluru, Mysore, Kochi, Mangalore, Trivandrum, Chennai, etc. to Kozhikode or Wayanad.
  • By air: Karipur International Airport at Kozhikode is the nearest airport to Wayanad. You can take a flight to Kozhikode, which is well connected to most cities in the country as well as the Middle East.

Getting around Wayanad:

  • Walking: All the trails have signboards marking the way and the locals are quite helpful.
  • Rickshaw: Hand-pulled rickshaws are available to hire. Walking is quicker than being pulled along in a rickshaw.
  • Visitors can choose the mode of transportation that suits their budget and preferences to reach Wayanad and explore the town.
