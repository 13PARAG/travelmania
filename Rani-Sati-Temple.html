Rani Sati Temple

Overview

The Rani Sati Temple in Jhunjhunu, Rajasthan, is a significant religious site dedicated to Rani Sati, a Rajasthani woman who lived between the 13th and 17th centuries and performed sati (self-immolation) when her husband died. The temple is the largest in India devoted to Rani Sati and is a pilgrimage site for millions of devotees from various religious backgrounds, including Hindu, Muslim, Sikh, Christian, and Jain. The temple complex houses thirteen sati temples, with the main temple being the largest and most prominent.The temple is unique for its lack of paintings or statues of female or male gods, instead, devotees worship a trishul representing power and might. The temple's architecture is colonial-Rajput style, featuring arched colonnade corridors, symmetrical cemented railings on the roof, and life-size statues of Lord Krishna, Goddess Lakshmi, and Goddess Bhawani, along with lion and elephant figurines.The temple's history is deeply rooted in the story of Rani Sati, who is also known as Narayani Devi and Dadiji. The legend behind Rani Sati's sati is a significant aspect of the temple's significance, with her act of self-immolation being a symbol of devotion and sacrifice. The temple trustees organize a well-attended annual fair to commemorate Rani Sati, attracting thousands of pilgrims.The Rani Sati Temple has been a subject of controversy, particularly in 1980 when a public procession taken out by Rani Sati devotees in New Delhi to celebrate the construction of a new Rani Sati temple led to discussions in both houses of the Indian Parliament over the promotion of the practice of sati. The Indian government issued an order proscribing the 'glorification of Sati', and tried banning the annual fair at Jhunjhunu. However, the temple and its traditions continue to be an essential part of the cultural and religious identity of the Marwari community and other communities in Rajasthan and beyond

Attractions

  • Main Temple: The main temple is the largest and most prominent in the temple complex, featuring a trishul representing power and might
  • Smaller Sati Temples: There are twelve smaller sati temples in the complex, each with its unique significance
  • Lord Hanuman Temple, Sita Temple, Thakur Ji temple, Lord Ganesha Temple and Shiva Temple: These temples are located within the Rani Sati temple complex and are notable for their exquisite murals and glass mosaics depicting the entire history of the place
  • Mangala Aarti and Sandhya Aarti: The temple performs an elaborate aarti two times a day, in the early morning and at sunset, which is a significant attraction for devotees
  • Bhadra Amavasya Poojan Utsav: The temple holds a special poojan utsav on the occasion of Bhadra Amavasya, which is of special significance to the temple

Accommodation

  • Shree Rani Satiji Mandir, Jhunjhunu: This is the temple itself, which has a rating of 4.8 and over 13,000 reviews. While not a hotel, it is a significant cultural and religious site in the area.
  • Neemrana's - Piramal Haveli Hotel, Jhunjhunu: This hotel is located about 30 minutes away from Rani Sati Temple and has received mixed reviews, with some guests praising its peaceful atmosphere and limited frescoes, while others have criticized its small size and distance from the main havelis in Mandawa.
  • Neelkanth Bagar Inn Resort, Chirawa: This resort is located in Chirawa, which is about an hour away from Rani Sati Temple. It has received positive reviews for its amenities and service, but its distance from the temple may be a drawback for some guests.



Transportation

Getting to Rani Sati Temple:

  • By Air: There is no direct flight connectivity to Jhunjhunu. To travel with flight, one has to take a flight only for Jaipur. Jaipur Airport is the nearest airport to Jhunjhunu which is located at a distance of about 185 km from Jhunjhunu. From here, one can easily reach the temple by using local bus or taxi
  • By Train: Traveling to the temple by train is the most preferred option as Jhunjhunu has its own railway junction which is located at a distance of about 2 km from the temple. One can easily reach the temple in about 20 minutes by auto, taxi or other local transport from outside the railway station
  • By Bus: Jhunjhunu is well connected by road to major nearby cities, and buses are a convenient mode of transportation. You can take a bus from nearby cities like Delhi, Jaipur, or Bikaner to reach Jhunjhunu and the temple.
  • By Road: Traveling to Rani Sati Temple Jhunjhunu by road is also very easy and convenient because Jhunjhunu is connected to most of the major nearby cities for Jhunjhunu, through which tourists can easily reach Jhunjhunu. One can also reach here by their personal car or by taxi

Getting Around Rani Sati Temple:

  • Timings: The temple is open from 5:00 AM to 1:00 PM and 3:00 PM to 10:00 PM
